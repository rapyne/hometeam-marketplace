Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about using HomeTeam.

How do I find the right practitioner?

Use our search and filter tools to browse practitioners by specialty, therapeutic approach, session type, and identity. Read their profiles, message them directly, and book an initial consultation to see if it's a good fit.

Are all practitioners verified?

Yes! Every practitioner on HomeTeam undergoes a thorough verification process. We verify their professional credentials, licensure, and commitment to providing inclusive, affirming care.

How much do sessions cost?

Session prices vary by practitioner and are displayed transparently on each profile. Many practitioners offer sliding scale fees, and you can filter by price range to find options within your budget. Sessions typically range from $75 to $250.

Do you accept insurance?

Insurance acceptance varies by practitioner. Each practitioner's profile lists which insurance plans they accept. We also provide superbills that you can submit to your insurance for potential reimbursement.

How do I become a practitioner on the platform?

Click "Join as Practitioner" and complete our application. We'll review your credentials, verify your licensure, and set up your profile. The process typically takes 3-5 business days.

Can I have virtual sessions?

Absolutely! Many of our practitioners offer telehealth sessions. You can filter for virtual-only practitioners in the search. Our platform supports secure video sessions to ensure your privacy.

What if I'm in crisis?

If you're in immediate danger, please call 911. For mental health crises, contact the 988 Suicide and Crisis Lifeline by calling or texting 988. The Crisis Text Line is also available — text HOME to 741741. HomeTeam is not a crisis service.

Provider FAQs

Who is HomeTeam for?
HomeTeam is for licensed mental health providers who want to support athletes during and beyond sport.
Do I need to be a former athlete to join?
No. But providers should understand athlete culture and the unique challenges athletes can face.
What kinds of providers is HomeTeam looking for?
HomeTeam is looking for licensed mental health providers who can support athletes through challenges like identity shifts, injury, performance pressure, transition, anxiety, depression, burnout, trauma, and eating concerns.
What is HomeTeam?
HomeTeam is a platform that helps athletes find trusted mental health providers who understand the realities of sport.
What makes HomeTeam different from other provider directories?
HomeTeam is focused on athlete mental health and athlete-centered care. We're not just building a directory — we're building a broader ecosystem to help athletes find support that fits the realities of sport.
Am I joining a group practice, or do I maintain my own practice?
Providers maintain their own independent practice. HomeTeam is not a group practice model.
Will I be able to decide whether a client is the right fit for my practice?
Yes. HomeTeam helps create better-fit connections, but providers still decide whether a prospective client is appropriate for their practice.
How do athletes find me?
Athletes can discover providers on HomeTeam through profile search, filtering, and matching. HomeTeam is also building partnership and visibility channels to help connect more athletes to care.
Can I offer virtual care only?
Yes. Providers may offer virtual care, in-person care, or both.
Can I join if I'm licensed in multiple states?
Yes. Providers will be able to indicate all applicable states on their profile.
What information will I need to submit?
Providers will be asked to share professional credentials, licensure details, practice information, and profile details.
How does the review process work?
Applications are reviewed for credentials, licensure, and overall fit with the platform.
Is there a cost to join?
There is no upfront cost for early providers joining HomeTeam. HomeTeam earns a revenue share only on clients who book and pay through the platform. Full terms are shared during onboarding.
